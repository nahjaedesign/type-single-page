History of the font

Roslindale, published in 2017 by David Jonathan Ross, is both a text and display variable serif that derives its inspiration from De Vinne, an 1890s typeface named after the famed nineteenth century printer. Influenced by the font’s Victorian sensibilities, Ross wanted to resurface the “diagonal stress” seen in many old styles. Roslindale provides a smooth, less clunky version of De Vinne that is able to be both dramatic and playful, while still holding true to its roots. Its strong contrast and “flirty” slickness, often associated with 70s typefaces such as ITC Bernase, inspired me to create this retro themed layout showcasing all the amazing things this font can do.
